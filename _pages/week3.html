    Thursday, May 30th - Episodic Memory (#11/28 of class):

    Learning Objectives:
    1. LO1: Continue to build a classroom culture & discuss science communication
    2. LO2: Apply principles of good science communication
    3. LO3: Describe the basic foundamental principles of episodic memory segmentation
    4. LO4: Summarize and critically analyze academic journal articles

    Agenda:

    1. Define logistics & discuss the scientist summary piece (LO1).
    2. Do a paragraph-by-paragraph breakdown of a scientist summary piece (LO1, LO2).
    3. Compare the science summary piece to the Journal of Neuroscience article: what does each do? How does the summary piece communicate the science? (LO2).
    4. Discuss basic theories on how episodic memory is segmented (i.e., what makes a memory its own event? How are memories 'constructed'?) (LO3).
    5. Critically discuss & analyze the academic journal articles (LO4).
    6. Reflections on class and any concerns/questions you, students, have.

    Articles:
    • Uitvlugt, M. G., & Healey, M. K. (2019). Temporal Proximity Links Unrelated News Events in Memory. Psychological Science, 30(1), 92–104. https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797618808474

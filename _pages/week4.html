    • Monday, June 3rd - Autobiographical Memory (#13/28 of class):

    Learning Objectives:
    1. LO1: Continue to build a classroom culture & discuss science communication
    2. LO2: Apply principles of good science communication
    3. LO3: Describe the basic foundamental principles of attention
    4. LO4: Summarize and critically analyze academic journal articles

    Agenda:

    1. Generate rubric for tweet summary assignment as a class (LO1).
    2. Apply list of science communication principles we generated to handouts with good and bad headlines and lead sentences & clarify headline/lead assignment (LO2).

    Friday, June 7th - Motivated Reasoning (#17/28 of class):

    Learning Objectives:
    1. LO1: Continue to build a classroom culture & discuss science communication.
    2. LO2: Describe the basic principles thought to underlie perception and perceptual illusions.
    3. LO3: Deconstruct and analyze the findings of an academic journal article.
    4. LO4: Compare and contrast models of science communication.

    Agenda:

    1. Discuss choosing Wikipedia scientists for assignment (LO1).
    2. Discuss the Psych Files podcast in relation to the rest: did it use good SciComm principles? What does our ideal podcast look like? (LO2)
    3. Review and critically analyze the methodology used in these papers & as discussed in the podcast here: what role is social media having on psych research? What are the ethics of this research? What kinds of questions can you ask?
    4. Critically discuss the papers: does it make sense that we are treating these concepts separate? What would an interdisciplinary approach look like to you?
    5. Reflections on class and any concerns/questions you, students, have (e.g., minute paper).

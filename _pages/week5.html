    • Monday, June 10th - Cognitive Biases (#18/28 of class):

    Learning Objectives:
    1. LO1: Continue to build a classroom culture & discuss science communication
    2. LO2: Apply principles of good science communication
    3. LO3: Describe the basic foundamental principles of various logical fallacies & cognitive biases
    4. LO4: Summarize and critically analyze academic journal articles

    Agenda:

    1. What does science communication research suggest to us, in light of cognitive biases? (LO2, LO3).
    2. Demos on various logical fallacies & dilemnas (e.g., Trolley problem) & discussion of this prior research (LO3).
    3. Discuss how this new podcast compares to previous podcasts in terms of our good science communication principles (LO2).
    4. How effective was the comic as a science communication tool? Compare and contrast the tools we have seen so far & whether you'd use them for different reasons (LO2).
    5. Summarize and analyze the journal articles on cognitive biases: can you think of a prevalence-induced concept change in your own life? (LO4).
    6. Reflections on class and any concerns/questions you, students, have.
     Readings

    Articles:
    • Levari, D. E., Gilbert, D. T., Wilson, T. D., Sievers, B., Amodio, D. M., & Wheatley, T. (2018). Prevalence-induced concept change in human judgment. Science, 360(6396), 1465–1467. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aap8731

    Friday, June 14th - Collective Memory (#22/28 of class):

    Learning Objectives:
    1. LO1: Continue to build a classroom culture & discuss science communication.
    2. LO2: Define, identify, and apply previous constructs that we have discussed in class
    3. LO3: Describe the basic principles thought to underlie collective memory.
    4. LO4: Deconstruct and analyze the findings of an academic journal article.
    5. LO5: Apply principles of good science communication.

    Agenda:

    1. Quiz on material covered since the last class quiz: short answers & multiple choice (LO2).
    2. Demo on collective memory (collective narcissism paper, plus paper not discussed); where does collective memory sit in the hierarchy of memory systems? (LO3)
    3. What do they think the main points of the collective memory article not covered were, based on the two science news papers? How effective was the science video used? (LO5)
    4. Critically discuss the papers: putting it all together, if memories close together in time are segmented together and if social media can be used to study affect labeling and the spread of fake news, how we can study fundamental principles of memory from history?
    5. Reflections on class and any concerns/questions you, students, have (e.g., minute paper).

    Articles:
